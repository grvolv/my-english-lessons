Unit 33 (32) Модальный глагол SHOULD | Курс английского для начинающих

OK English - уроки английского языка

 

  1. Значение и использование:
    • Модальный глагол "should" применяется для выражения советов, рекомендаций, моральных обязательств или для указания на желательное действие в настоящем или будущем. Обычно переводится как «следует» или «должен».
    • Например, в ситуации, когда необходимо предложить что-то полезное или правильное действие, конструкция "You should..." помогает выразить рекомендацию или совет.
  2. Типовые структуры предложений:
    • Утверждения: "You should study harder for your exams."
    • Отрицания: "You shouldn't ignore your health."
    • Вопросы: "Should I call him tomorrow?"
  3. Часто употребляемые случаи:
    • Давать советы: "You should exercise regularly."
    • Предлагать рекомендации или варианты: "I think you should try the new restaurant downtown."
    • Выражать мнение: "Should" часто используется для вежливого выражения мнения: "You should apologize to him."
  4. Отличия от других модальных глаголов:
    • "Should" отличается от "must" тем, что не указывает на обязательство, а скорее на рекомендацию. Также используется мягче, чем "have to", который подразумевает более серьезную необходимость.

Тем временем их сайт "ранжировался совершенно нормально" в других поисковых системах, но, по словам Шиваун, это не имело особого значения, так как "все пользуются Google".

 

The couple would later discover that their site was not the only one to have been put at a disadvantage by Google – by the time the tech giant was found guilty and fined in 2017 there were around 20 claimants, including Kelkoo, Trivago and Yelp.

Позже пара узнала, что их сайт был не единственным, кто оказался в невыгодном положении из-за Google — к моменту, когда техногигант был признан виновным и оштрафован в 2017 году, было около 20 истцов, включая Kelkoo, Trivago и Yelp.

 

EU court rules Google must pay €2.4bn fine

Суд ЕС постановил, что Google должен заплатить штраф в размере 2,4 млрд евро

 

Adam, who had built a career in supercomputing, says he had the "eureka moment" for Foundem while smoking a cigarette outside the offices of his previous employer.

Адам, сделавший карьеру в области суперкомпьютеров, говорит, что идея для Foundem пришла к нему как "озарение", когда он курил сигарету возле офиса своего предыдущего работодателя.

 

Then, price comparison websites were in their infancy, and each specialised in one particular product. But Foundem was different because it let customers compare a large range of products – from clothes to flights.

Тогда сайты для сравнения цен только зарождались, и каждый из них специализировался на одном конкретном продукте. Но Foundem был уникальным, так как позволял клиентам сравнивать широкий ассортимент товаров — от одежды до авиабилетов.

 

“No-one else was anywhere close to this,” beams Shivaun, who herself had been a software consultant for several major global brands.

"Никто не предлагал ничего подобного," — с гордостью говорит Шиваун, которая сама работала консультантом по программному обеспечению для нескольких крупных мировых брендов.

 

In its 2017 judgement, the European Commission found that Google had illegally promoted its own comparison shopping service in search results, whilst demoting those of competitors.

В своём постановлении 2017 года Европейская комиссия установила, что Google незаконно продвигал свою собственную службу сравнения цен в результатах поиска, при этом понижая сайты конкурентов.

Ten years before that, though – when Foundem launched - Adam says he had no reason to assume Google was being deliberately anti-competitive over online shopping. "They weren't really serious players," he says.

Однако десять лет назад — в момент запуска Foundem — Адам утверждает, что у него не было причин полагать, что Google намеренно создаёт антиконкурентные условия в онлайн-шоппинге. "Они не были серьёзными игроками," — говорит он.

 

But by the end of 2008, the couple had started to suspect foul play.

Но к концу 2008 года у пары появились подозрения, что что-то нечисто.

 

It was three weeks before Christmas and the pair received a message warning that their website had suddenly become slow to load. They thought it was a cyber attack, "but actually it was just that everyone had started visiting our website", Adam laughs.

Это было за три недели до Рождества, и пара получила сообщение о том, что их сайт вдруг стал медленно загружаться. Они подумали, что это кибератака, "но на самом деле это просто все начали заходить на наш сайт," — смеётся Адам.

 

Channel 5’s The Gadget Show had just named Foundem the best price comparison website in the UK.

Программа The Gadget Show на канале Channel 5 только что назвала Foundem лучшим сайтом для сравнения цен в Великобритании.

 

“And that was really important,” Shivaun explains, "because we then reached out to Google and said, look, surely it's not benefiting your users to make it impossible for them to find us.

"И это было действительно важно," — объясняет Шиваун, "потому что тогда мы обратились к Google и сказали, что, возможно, пользователям невыгодно, что они не могут нас найти."

 

“And that still got from Google, not a complete ignore, but a basically ‘bog off’.”

"И от Google мы всё ещё получали не полное игнорирование, но что-то вроде 'отвяжитесь от нас'."

 

"That was the moment we knew, OK, we need to fight," says Adam.

"В этот момент мы поняли: хорошо, нам нужно бороться," — говорит Адам.

 

The couple went to the press, with limited success, and took their case to regulators in the UK, US, and Brussels.

Пара обратилась в прессу, но успех был ограниченным, и они подали жалобы регуляторам в Великобритании, США и Брюсселе.

 

It was in the latter – with the European Commission (EC) – that the case eventually took off, with the launch of an antitrust investigation in November 2010. The couple’s first meeting with the regulators took place in a portable cabin in Brussels.

Именно в последнем случае — в Европейской комиссии (ЕК) — их дело получило ход с началом антимонопольного расследования в ноябре 2010 года. Первая встреча пары с регуляторами состоялась в передвижном офисе в Брюсселе.

 

“One of the things they said was if this is a systemic issue, why are you the first people we’re seeing?” Shivaun recalls. “We said we're not 100% sure, but we suspect people are afraid, because all businesses on the internet essentially rely on Google for the lifeblood that is their traffic."

"Одна из вещей, которую они сказали, была: если это системная проблема, почему вы первые, кого мы видим?" — вспоминает Шиваун. "Мы ответили, что не уверены на 100%, но подозреваем, что люди боятся, потому что все интернет-бизнесы фактически зависят от Google, который является источником их трафика."

 

'We don't like bullies'

'Мы не любим хулиганов'

 

The couple were in a hotel room in Brussels, only a few hundred yards from the commission building, when competition commissioner Margarethe Vestager finally announced the verdict that they, and other shopping websites, had been waiting for.

Пара находилась в гостиничном номере в Брюсселе, всего в нескольких сотнях метров от здания комиссии, когда комиссар по конкуренции Маргрет Вестагер наконец объявила вердикт, которого они и другие сайты ждали.

 

But there was no popping of champagne corks. Their focus then turned to making sure the EC enforced its decision.

Но не было никаких хлопков шампанского. Их внимание теперь сосредоточилось на том, чтобы убедиться, что ЕК исполнит своё решение.

 

"I guess it was unfortunate for Google that they did it to us," Shivaun says. "We've both been brought up maybe under the delusion that we can make a difference, and we really don't like bullies."

"Полагаю, Google не повезло, что они сделали это с нами," — говорит Шиваун. "Мы оба, наверное, выросли в иллюзии, что можем что-то изменить, и мы действительно не любим хулиганов."

Even Google’s final defeat in the case last month did not spell the end for the couple.

Даже окончательное поражение Google в этом деле в прошлом месяце не стало концом для пары.

 

They believe Google’s conduct remains anti-competitive and the EC is looking into it. In March this year, under its new Digital Markets Act, the commission opened an investigation into Google’s parent company, Alphabet, over whether it continues to preference its own goods and services in search results.

Они считают, что поведение Google остаётся антиконкурентным, и ЕК продолжает его изучать. В марте этого года в рамках нового Закона о цифровых рынках комиссия начала расследование в отношении материнской компании Google — Alphabet — чтобы выяснить, продолжает ли она продвигать свои собственные товары и услуги в результатах поиска.

 

A spokesperson for Google said: “The CJEU [European Court of Justice] judgment [in 2024] only relates to how we showed product results from 2008-2017.

Представитель Google заявил: "Решение Суда Европейского Союза (CJEU) [в 2024 году] касается только того, как мы показывали результаты продуктов в период с 2008 по 2017 годы."

 

"The changes we made in 2017 to comply with the European Commission's Shopping decision have worked successfully for more than seven years, generating billions of clicks for more than 800 comparison shopping services.

"Изменения, которые мы внесли в 2017 году, чтобы соответствовать решению Европейской комиссии по покупкам, успешно работают уже более семи лет, обеспечивая миллиарды кликов для более чем 800 сервисов сравнения цен."

 

"For this reason, we continue to strongly contest the claims made by Foundem and will do so when the case is considered by the courts."

"По этой причине мы продолжаем решительно оспаривать претензии Foundem и будем делать это, когда дело будет рассмотрено в судах."

 

The Raffs are also pursuing a civil damages claim against Google, which is due to begin in the first half of 2026. But when, or if, a final victory comes for the couple it will likely be a Pyrrhic one - they were forced to close Foundem in 2016.

Раффы также подали гражданский иск о возмещении ущерба против Google, который должен начаться в первой половине 2026 года. Но даже если окончательная победа для пары когда-либо наступит, это, вероятно, будет пиррова победа — они были вынуждены закрыть Foundem в 2016 году.

 

The long fight against Google has been gruelling for them, too. "I think if we had known it was going to be quite as many years as it turned out to be we might not have made the same choice," Adam admits.

Долгая борьба с Google была для них также изнурительной. "Думаю, если бы мы знали, что это займёт столько лет, возможно, мы бы не сделали тот же выбор," — признаётся Адам.

  1. Sentence:

"It was three weeks before Christmas and the pair received a message warning that their website had suddenly become slow to load."

    • Type: Compound-Complex Sentence
    • Conjunctions: Coordinating conjunction "and," subordinating conjunction "that."
    • Main and Subordinate Clauses:
      • Main Clause 1: It was three weeks before Christmas.
      • Main Clause 2: The pair received a message warning.
      • Subordinate Clause: that their website had suddenly become slow to load.
      • Dependent part: "that their website had suddenly become slow to load" (dependent on “received a message warning”).
    • Grammatical Structure:
      • Main Clause 1:
        • Subject: "It" (dummy subject).
        • Predicate: "was" (verb, simple past).
        • Adverbial: "three weeks before Christmas" (time).
      • Main Clause 2:
        • Subject: "the pair."
        • Predicate: "received" (verb, simple past).
        • Direct Object: "a message warning."
      • Subordinate Clause (Indirect Object of "received a message warning"):
        • Subject: "their website."
        • Predicate: "had become slow to load" (verb phrase, past perfect, indicating completed state).
        • Adverbial Modifier: "suddenly."
    • Parts of Speech and Functions:
      • "It": dummy subject, placeholder.
      • "was": verb, main action in Main Clause 1.
      • "received": main verb, denotes action.
      • "that": conjunction introducing a subordinate clause.
    • Tenses and Aspects:
      • Main clauses in Simple Past.
      • Subordinate clause in Past Perfect (indicating a state that became apparent before the main event).
    • Special Construction: Use of "It was" as a time marker.

 

  1. Sentence:

"The couple’s trailblazing price comparison website Foundem - one they had sacrificed well-paid jobs for and built from scratch - had just gone fully live."

    • Type: Complex Sentence with appositive phrase
    • Main and Subordinate Clauses:
      • Main Clause: The couple’s trailblazing price comparison website Foundem had just gone fully live.
      • Subordinate Clause (non-defining relative clause): one they had sacrificed well-paid jobs for and built from scratch.
      • Main Part: "Foundem had just gone fully live" (independent clause).
      • Dependent Part: "one they had sacrificed... and built from scratch."
    • Grammatical Structure:
      • Main Clause:
        • Subject: "The couple’s trailblazing price comparison website Foundem."
        • Predicate: "had gone fully live" (verb phrase, past perfect).
        • Adverbial Modifier: "just" (indicating immediacy).
      • Subordinate Clause:
        • Subject: "they" (refers to "the couple").
        • Predicate: "had sacrificed" and "built" (verb phrases in past perfect, connected by "and").
        • Direct Object: "well-paid jobs" (sacrificed) and implicit object "it" (built).
    • Parts of Speech and Functions:
      • "had gone": main verb in past perfect, indicating completion.
      • "just": adverb, modifying "gone."
      • "for": preposition, linking "sacrificed" with "jobs."
    • Tenses and Aspects:
      • Both main and subordinate clauses use Past Perfect (to describe completed actions before the main event).
    • Special Construction: Appositive phrase giving additional context.

 

  1. Sentence:

"If you’re denied traffic, then you have no business."

    • Type: Complex Sentence (Conditional)
    • Conjunctions: Subordinating conjunction "if."
    • Main and Subordinate Clauses:
      • Main Clause: you have no business.
      • Subordinate Clause (conditional): If you’re denied traffic.
      • Dependent part: "If you’re denied traffic" (conditional, depends on main clause).
    • Grammatical Structure:
      • Main Clause:
        • Subject: "you."
        • Predicate: "have" (verb, simple present).
        • Direct Object: "no business."
      • Subordinate Clause:
        • Subject: "you."
        • Predicate: "are denied" (passive verb phrase in present simple).
        • Direct Object: "traffic."
    • Parts of Speech and Functions:
      • "if": conjunction, introduces conditional clause.
      • "denied": past participle, used in passive form.
    • Tenses and Aspects:
      • Both clauses in Simple Present.
    • Special Construction: Passive voice used for "are denied," highlighting the subject's experience rather than action.

 

  1. Sentence:

"Google spent seven years fighting that verdict, issued in June 2017, but in September this year Europe’s top court – the European Court of Justice – rejected its appeals."

    • Type: Compound-Complex Sentence
    • Conjunctions: Coordinating conjunction "but."
    • Main and Subordinate Clauses:
      • Main Clause 1: Google spent seven years fighting that verdict.
      • Main Clause 2: Europe’s top court rejected its appeals.
      • Subordinate Clause: issued in June 2017 (non-defining relative clause, modifies "verdict").
    • Grammatical Structure:
      • Main Clause 1:
        • Subject: "Google."
        • Predicate: "spent fighting" (verb phrase, simple past).
        • Direct Object: "seven years fighting that verdict."
      • Main Clause 2:
        • Subject: "Europe’s top court."
        • Predicate: "rejected" (verb, simple past).
        • Direct Object: "its appeals."
    • Parts of Speech and Functions:
      • "spent": main verb, indicates time spent.
      • "fighting": gerund, describes activity.
      • "but": coordinating conjunction, joins contrasting ideas.
    • Tenses and Aspects:
      • Simple Past in both main clauses, describing completed actions.
    • Special Construction: Gerund phrase "fighting that verdict" as part of the object of "spent."

"If we had known it was going to be quite as many years as it turned out to be we might not have made the same choice."

    • Main and Subordinate Clauses:
      • Main Clause: we might not have made the same choice.
      • Subordinate Clause: If we had known it was going to be quite as many years as it turned out to be.
      • Dependent part: "If we had known…" (conditional clause).
    • Grammatical Structure:
      • Main Clause:
        • Subject: "we."
        • Predicate: "might not have made" (modal verb phrase, perfect aspect).
        • Direct Object: "the same choice."
      • Subordinate Clause:
        • Subject: "we."
        • Predicate: "had known" (verb in past perfect).
        • Complement: "it was going to be quite as many years as it turned out to be."
    • Parts of Speech and Functions:
      • "If": conjunction, introduces conditional.
      • "might not have made": modal verb phrase indicating hypothetical past action.
    • Tenses and Aspects:
      • Past Perfect in subordinate clause, conditional Perfect in main clause (showing hypothetical action).
    • Special Construction: Use of third conditional for hypothetical reflection.

  1. Reading Comprehension: 5 Questions with Answers

Q1: Why did Shivaun and Adam Raff begin a legal battle against Google?

A1: Shivaun and Adam Raff started a legal battle against Google because their website, Foundem, was penalized in Google’s search results, making it nearly invisible to users. This negatively impacted their business, as most online traffic came through Google.

Q2: What was Foundem's main purpose as a website?

A2: Foundem was a price comparison website that allowed users to compare a wide range of products, from clothes to flights.

Q3: How did the European Commission (EC) respond to the Raffs’ case against Google?

A3: The European Commission launched an antitrust investigation into Google, eventually concluding that Google had abused its market dominance by promoting its own services over competitors.

Q4: What was the outcome of the European Court of Justice’s ruling on Google’s appeal?

A4: The European Court of Justice rejected Google’s appeal, upholding the previous ruling and the fine imposed by the European Commission.

Q5: What is the current status of the Raffs’ fight against Google?

A5: Despite their legal victory, the Raffs continue to pursue a civil damages claim against Google, which is scheduled to begin in 2026. They also believe Google’s conduct remains anti-competitive.

Topic: Discussing the Legal Battle Against Google and Its Implications

Person A: Hi! Have you heard about the couple that fought against Google and won a huge case?

Person B: Yes, I read about it! It’s incredible that they managed to take on such a giant company.

Person A: Absolutely. They started a price comparison website called Foundem, but Google’s search algorithm penalized them.

Person B: I can’t imagine how frustrating that must have been, especially since they had invested so much in their startup.

Person A: Exactly. They lost their visibility online and almost no one could find their website.

Person B: So, they decided to file a legal complaint against Google, right?

Person A: Yes, they did. It wasn’t easy; it took them around 15 years to reach a final verdict.

Person B: That’s such a long time! Did they get any support along the way?

Person A: They had support from other companies like Kelkoo and Yelp, who also felt that Google’s practices were unfair.

Person B: I read that the European Commission fined Google around 2 billion pounds. That’s massive!

Person A: Yes, it’s a record-breaking fine. Google appealed multiple times, but the courts upheld the decision.

Person B: I wonder if this will change how big tech companies operate in the future.

Person A: Hopefully. This case has been a landmark for regulating Big Tech in Europe.

Person B: Do you think it’s possible for a small company to survive against such giants nowadays?

Person A: It’s challenging, but this case shows it’s possible if you’re persistent enough.

Person B: True. The Raffs had to close Foundem in 2016, though. I think it was too hard to sustain their business.

Person A: Yes, it’s unfortunate. Still, they’re pushing for a civil damages claim against Google.

Person B: Do you think they’ll win this time too?

Person A: Hard to say, but I think they have a strong case. Google’s practices were clearly unfair.

Person B: It’ll be interesting to see how this case impacts the tech industry. I’ll definitely keep an eye on it.

Person A: Same here. Let’s hope it brings more transparency to online business practices.

Основные моменты теории

  1. Значение глагола "should":
    • "Should" используется для выражения совета, рекомендации или мнения о том, что правильно сделать в определённой ситуации. В русском переводе это часто означает «следует сделать» или «не следует делать».
    • Пример из видео: "You should study all units of Red Murphy" — «Вам следует выучить все юниты по Красному Мёрфи».
  2. Структура предложений с "should":
    • После подлежащего идёт "should", затем основной глагол в начальной форме (инфинитив) без частицы "to".
    • В отрицательных предложениях используется "shouldn't" для указания на то, что делать не рекомендуется.
    • Пример: "You shouldn’t watch TV so much" — «Тебе не следует так много смотреть телевизор».
  3. Использование "should" для выражения мнения:
    • "Should" часто применяется для выражения субъективного мнения говорящего. Это мягкий совет или мнение о правильном действии, которое, по мнению говорящего, будет наилучшим вариантом.
    • Пример: "I think you should buy this coat" — «Я думаю, что тебе следует купить это пальто».
  4. Сравнение с "must":
    • "Should" мягче, чем "must", который подразумевает более строгую обязанность или настоятельный призыв к действию.
    • Пример: "It’s a good movie, you should see it" — это мягкая рекомендация; в отличие от "must", который звучал бы как требование.
  5. Связка "I think + should":
    • Используется для выражения мнения говорящего. Если нужно выразить несогласие, отрицание ставится перед "think": "I don’t think you should."
    • Пример: "I don’t think you should work so hard" — «Я не думаю, что тебе следует так усердно работать».
