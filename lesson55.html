Раздел 1. Грамматика

  1. Общие сведения о глаголе get
    Глагол get является фразовым глаголом, который имеет много значений, зависящих от контекста. Наиболее распространённые значения включают "получить", "становиться", "добираться до" и "готовить" что-либо. В сочетаниях с предлогами или наречиями get может приобретать совершенно новые значения, и эти фразовые глаголы нужно запоминать.
  2. Структура предложений с get
  • Утвердительные предложения:
    Подлежащее + get(s) + дополнение
    Пример: "I get a bonus at work every year."
  • Отрицательные предложения:
    Подлежащее + do not (don’t) get / does not (doesn’t) get + дополнение
    Пример: "He doesn’t get enough sleep."
  • Вопросительные предложения:
    Do/Does + подлежащее + get + дополнение?
    Пример: "Do you get along with your neighbors?"
  1. Использование в разных ситуациях
  • Для обозначения получения или приобретения:
    Пример: "I got a gift from my friend."
  • Для выражения изменения состояния:
    Пример: "It's getting dark outside."
  • Для обозначения прибытия в определённое место:
    Пример: "They got to the airport in time."
  1. Фразовые глаголы с get
  • get up – вставать, пробуждаться: "I get up early every morning."
  • get over – преодолевать, восстанавливаться: "She got over her fear of heights."
  • get along – ладить, уживаться: "The new colleagues get along really well."
  • get away – уезжать, спасаться: "They got away for a weekend trip."
  • get by – справляться, выживать: "I can get by with my basic French."
  1. Когда использовать get
  • Вместо "become" для описания изменений:
    Пример: "She got angry when she heard the news."
  • Для обозначения действия получения или приобретения:
    Пример: "Did you get my email yesterday?"
  • Для выражения достижения или прибытия:
    Пример: "We need to get to the station before 6 p.m."
  1. Особенности использования
  • В повседневной речи get часто используется для упрощения выражений.
  • Важно различать get и более формальные глаголы, такие как "receive", "arrive", "become", которые могут быть предпочтительны в формальных контекстах.
  1. Важные моменты для изучения
  • Обратите внимание на правильное употребление get в разных ситуациях.
  • Изучите основные фразовые глаголы с get, так как они помогают разнообразить речь.
  • Практикуйте произношение и использование get в повседневных ситуациях, чтобы звучать естественно.

Section 1. Grammar

  1. General Information about the Verb get
    The verb get is a phrasal verb with multiple meanings depending on the context. The most common meanings include "to receive," "to become," "to reach," and "to prepare" something. When combined with prepositions or adverbs, get takes on completely new meanings, and these phrasal verbs must be memorized.
  2. Sentence Structure with get
  • Affirmative Sentences:
    Subject + get(s) + object
    Example: "I get a bonus at work every year."
  • Negative Sentences:
    Subject + do not (don’t) get / does not (doesn’t) get + object
    Example: "He doesn’t get enough sleep."
  • Interrogative Sentences:
    Do/Does + subject + get + object?
    Example: "Do you get along with your neighbors?"
  1. Usage in Different Situations
  • To indicate receiving or obtaining:
    Example: "I got a gift from my friend."
  • To express a change of state:
    Example: "It's getting dark outside."
  • To denote reaching a certain place:
    Example: "They got to the airport in time."
  1. Phrasal Verbs with get
  • get up – to get out of bed: "I get up early every morning."
  • get over – to recover from something: "She got over her fear of heights."
  • get along – to get on well: "The new colleagues get along really well."
  • get away – to escape or go on vacation: "They got away for a weekend trip."
  • get by – to manage or survive: "I can get by with my basic French."
  1. When to Use get
  • Instead of "become" to describe changes:
    Example: "She got angry when she heard the news."
  • To express the action of receiving or obtaining:
    Example: "Did you get my email yesterday?"
  • To indicate reaching or arriving:
    Example: "We need to get to the station before 6 p.m."
  1. Usage Features
  • Get is often used in everyday speech to simplify expressions.
  • It is crucial to distinguish between get and more formal verbs, such as "receive," "arrive," "become," which may be preferable in formal contexts.
  1. Key Points to Study
  • Pay attention to the correct usage of get in various contexts.
  • Learn the essential phrasal verbs with get to make your speech more diverse.
  • Practice pronunciation and using get in daily situations to sound more natural.

Фразовые глаголы с get могут значительно менять значение основного глагола. Вот список некоторых популярных фразовых глаголов с get и их значения:

  • get up – вставать, подниматься с постели:
    Пример: "I usually get up at 6 a.m. on weekdays."
  • get over – преодолевать, выздоравливать:
    Пример: "It took her months to get over the breakup."
  • get along (with) – ладить, уживаться с кем-то:
    Пример: "They get along with each other really well."
  • get away – уезжать, убегать, выбираться на отдых:
    Пример: "We are planning to get away for the weekend."
  • get by – справляться, выживать:
    Пример: "He doesn’t earn much, but they manage to get by."
  • get down – расстраивать, угнетать:
    Пример: "The gloomy weather always gets me down."
  • get into – попасть, ввязаться (в ситуацию, автомобиль, и т.д.):
    Пример: "She got into trouble for skipping school."
  • get out – выйти, выбраться:
    Пример: "Let’s get out of here before it starts to rain."
  • get back – вернуться:
    Пример: "I’ll get back from my trip on Sunday."
  • get through – пройти через что-то, справиться:
    Пример: "She got through a lot of hardships in her life."
  • get on – садиться (на транспорт), продолжать:
    Пример: "We need to get on the bus quickly." / "Let’s get on with the project."
  • get off – выходить (из транспорта), отделаться:
    Пример: "We got off the train at the last station."

  1. Phrasal Verbs with get

Phrasal verbs with get can change the meaning of the main verb significantly. Here is a list of some common phrasal verbs with get and their meanings:

  • get up – to get out of bed:
    Example: "I usually get up at 6 a.m. on weekdays."
  • get over – to overcome, recover:
    Example: "It took her months to get over the breakup."
  • get along (with) – to get on well with someone:
    Example: "They get along with each other really well."
  • get away – to leave, escape, or go on vacation:
    Example: "We are planning to get away for the weekend."
  • get by – to manage or survive:
    Example: "He doesn’t earn much, but they manage to get by."
  • get down – to depress or make someone sad:
    Example: "The gloomy weather always gets me down."
  • get into – to get involved in something or enter:
    Example: "She got into trouble for skipping school."
  • get out – to leave or escape:
    Example: "Let’s get out of here before it starts to rain."
  • get back – to return:
    Example: "I’ll get back from my trip on Sunday."
  • get through – to endure or manage to deal with something:
    Example: "She got through a lot of hardships in her life."
  • get on – to board (a vehicle), to continue:
    Example: "We need to get on the bus quickly." / "Let’s get on with the project."
  • get off – to disembark or avoid trouble:
    Example: "We got off the train at the last station."
