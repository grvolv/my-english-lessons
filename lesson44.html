Урок 44: Вопрос к подлежащему (со словом who и what)

Lesson 44: Subject Questions in English Using "Who" and "What"

https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=uRrOlibYr34&list=PLYB0SmefqEsniU1UbGzrfhNCV3noALHj7&index=91

  1. Introduction

Subject questions in English are a type of question where "who" or "what" functions as the subject of the sentence. Unlike other questions that require auxiliary verbs, subject questions do not use auxiliary verbs. Understanding subject questions helps in forming questions that focus on "who" or "what" is performing the action.

  1. Rules of Subject Questions
  2. Structure: Subject questions do not need auxiliary verbs (like "do," "does," "did").
    • Example: Who made this cake? (Correct)
    • Incorrect: Who did make this cake?
  3. Verb Agreement: The verb agrees with the singular or plural form of "who" or "what."
    • Example: What causes this reaction? (Singular)
    • Example: Who are attending the meeting? (Plural)
  4. No Inversion: Do not invert the subject and verb in subject questions.
    • Example: Who took my book? (Correct)
    • Incorrect: Who did take my book?
  5. Use with Tenses: Subject questions can be used in all tenses, but the structure remains simple.
  6. Context: Use these questions when the focus is on the subject performing the action.
  7. Examples of Subject Questions

Present Simple:

  1. Who plays the guitar?
  2. What makes you happy?
  3. Who writes the best stories?
  4. What causes earthquakes?
  5. Who loves chocolate ice cream?

Past Simple:

  1. Who won the match yesterday?
  2. What started the fire?
  3. Who painted this portrait?
  4. What made you change your mind?
  5. Who called you last night?
  6. Explanation of Usage
  • When to Use: Use subject questions when you are interested in identifying the subject of an action. These questions are straightforward and do not require an auxiliary verb.
  • Why They Are Different: Unlike object questions, where the subject is already known and the auxiliary verb is needed, subject questions directly focus on the unknown subject, making the structure simpler.
  • Key Points:
    • No auxiliary verbs
    • Verb agrees with the subject
    • Keep the sentence structure simple
  1. Important Aspects to Focus On
  2. Subject-Verb Agreement: Make sure the verb correctly matches the subject in number and tense.
  3. Avoid Inversion: Remember that subject questions do not follow the usual question format.
  4. Clarity in Context: Use subject questions only when the subject is unknown.
  5. Additional Notes
  • Singular vs. Plural: Pay attention to whether the subject is singular or plural and use the correct form of the verb.
  • Practice with Different Tenses: Try forming subject questions in different tenses to strengthen your understanding.

Analysis of the Text: "Kate has been amazing, Prince William says"

The original article from the BBC discusses a statement by Prince William praising his wife, Kate Middleton, for her resilience, support, and dedication. The focus of the piece is on the impact and significance of Kate’s role within the royal family, emphasizing her work in various charitable endeavors and her influence as a public figure. This text serves as an excellent basis for exploring key vocabulary related to public service, family dynamics, and the duties of royalty. Additionally, it presents an opportunity to analyze complex sentence structures, including reported speech and descriptive clauses, which are important for language learners at the C1 level.

Text Based on the Theme

Paragraph 1

Prince William has openly praised his wife, Catherine, Princess of Wales, highlighting her unwavering dedication to their family and the numerous causes she supports. In a recent interview, he described her as “amazing” for the way she has handled the pressures and responsibilities that come with being a senior member of the British royal family. This public acknowledgment sheds light on the significant yet often underappreciated work that Kate undertakes behind the scenes.

Paragraph 2

Throughout her time as a royal, Kate Middleton has evolved into a confident and influential public figure. From championing mental health awareness to advocating for early childhood development, she has taken on a wide array of projects that reflect her personal passions. These initiatives not only serve the public but also demonstrate her commitment to using her platform for meaningful impact.

Paragraph 3

One of Kate’s most notable efforts is her work in early childhood education. Recognizing the critical role that the first few years of life play in a person’s development, she has collaborated with experts and charities to promote better understanding and support for young children and their families. Her involvement in this field has sparked important conversations and inspired action at a national level.

Paragraph 4

The princess's advocacy for mental health is another area where she has made a profound difference. Alongside Prince William and Prince Harry, Kate co-founded the Heads Together campaign, which aims to end the stigma surrounding mental health. Her willingness to discuss the challenges of parenting and mental well-being has resonated with many and has helped bring these important issues into the mainstream.

Paragraph 5

Balancing public duties and family life is no small feat, yet Kate seems to handle it with grace. As a mother of three, she has often spoken about the joys and difficulties of raising children, even sharing personal anecdotes to connect with other parents. Her relatable approach and genuine concern for the well-being of young families have endeared her to the public.

Paragraph 6

Despite the constant media attention, Kate has managed to maintain a sense of normalcy for her children. Both she and Prince William are known for prioritizing their family life, striving to give their children experiences similar to those of any other child in the UK. This commitment to family values, combined with their royal obligations, exemplifies the couple’s desire to modernize the monarchy.

Paragraph 7

Prince William’s admiration for Kate extends beyond her public role; he often speaks of her strength and reliability in their private life. Their partnership, built on mutual respect and shared values, continues to evolve as they navigate the complexities of royal life. Kate’s ability to balance her responsibilities while remaining a supportive wife and mother has undoubtedly played a crucial role in shaping the future of the British monarchy.

Grammar Analysis of 5 Complex Sentences

  1. Sentence: "Prince William has openly praised his wife, Catherine, Princess of Wales, highlighting her unwavering dedication to their family and the numerous causes she supports."
    • Type: Complex
    • Conjunctions: No conjunctions; uses a participial phrase "highlighting" as a modifier.
    • Main Clause: Prince William has openly praised his wife
    • Subordinate Part: highlighting her unwavering dedication to their family and the numerous causes she supports (participial phrase modifying the main clause)
    • Subject: Prince William
    • Predicate: has praised
    • Direct Object: his wife, Catherine, Princess of Wales
    • Adverbial: openly
    • Participial Phrase: highlighting her unwavering dedication (acts as an adjective)
    • Parts of Speech:
      • praised (verb, present perfect)
      • wife, dedication, causes (nouns)
      • highlighting (present participle)
      • supports (verb, present simple)
    • Tense and Aspect: Present Perfect (main verb), Present Simple (supports)
    • Function: The participial phrase describes the result or effect of William's praise.
    • Structure: Subject + Predicate + Direct Object + Participial Phrase.
  2. Sentence: "Throughout her time as a royal, Kate Middleton has evolved into a confident and influential public figure."
    • Type: Simple
    • Conjunctions: None
    • Subject: Kate Middleton
    • Predicate: has evolved
    • Adverbial: Throughout her time as a royal (prepositional phrase)
    • Object Complement: into a confident and influential public figure
    • Parts of Speech:
      • has evolved (verb, present perfect)
      • confident, influential (adjectives)
      • figure (noun)
    • Tense and Aspect: Present Perfect
    • Function: The adverbial modifies the verb, and the object complement describes what Kate Middleton has become.
    • Structure: Subject + Predicate + Object Complement.
  3. Sentence: "Her involvement in this field has sparked important conversations and inspired action at a national level."
    • Type: Compound
    • Conjunctions: and
    • Main Clauses: Her involvement in this field has sparked important conversations + inspired action at a national level
    • Subject: Her involvement
    • Predicate: has sparked, inspired
    • Direct Objects: important conversations, action
    • Adverbial: at a national level
    • Parts of Speech:
      • has sparked, inspired (verbs, present perfect)
      • important (adjective)
      • action (noun)
    • Tense and Aspect: Present Perfect
    • Function: Two main clauses joined by "and," describing the results of her involvement.
    • Structure: Subject + Predicate + Direct Objects + Adverbial.
  4. Sentence: "Despite the constant media attention, Kate has managed to maintain a sense of normalcy for her children."
    • Type: Complex
    • Conjunctions: Despite (preposition)
    • Main Clause: Kate has managed to maintain a sense of normalcy for her children
    • Subordinate Part: Despite the constant media attention (prepositional phrase)
    • Subject: Kate
    • Predicate: has managed to maintain
    • Direct Object: a sense of normalcy
    • Prepositional Phrase: for her children
    • Parts of Speech:
      • has managed (verb, present perfect)
      • normalcy (noun)
      • for (preposition)
    • Tense and Aspect: Present Perfect
    • Function: The prepositional phrase introduces a contrast to the main clause.
    • Structure: Subordinate Phrase + Main Clause.
  5. Sentence: "Their partnership, built on mutual respect and shared values, continues to evolve as they navigate the complexities of royal life."
    • Type: Complex
    • Conjunctions: as
    • Main Clause: Their partnership continues to evolve
    • Subordinate Clause: as they navigate the complexities of royal life
    • Subject: Their partnership
    • Predicate: continues to evolve
    • Adverbial Clause: as they navigate the complexities of royal life
    • Parts of Speech:
      • continues (verb, present simple)
      • built (past participle, describes "partnership")
      • navigate (verb, present simple)
    • Tense and Aspect: Present Simple
    • Function: The adverbial clause indicates the timing of the main action.
    • Structure: Subject + Predicate + Adverbial Clause.
