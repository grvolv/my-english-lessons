  1. Introduction
  • Objective: Understand and use English questions with "what," "which," and "how" correctly.
  • Overview: This lesson focuses on forming and using questions with "what," "which," and "how," discussing their nuances and contexts.
  1. Explanation of the Rule
  • What: Used when asking about something unspecified or open-ended.
    • Example: What is your favorite color?
  • Which: Used when choosing among limited options.
    • Example: Which dress do you prefer — the red one or the blue one?
  • How: Used to inquire about the manner, condition, or degree.
    • Example: How did you solve this problem?
  1. Structure of Questions
  • What + Verb + Subject?
    • Example: What does he want?
  • Which + Noun + Verb + Subject?
    • Example: Which movie would you like to watch?
  • How + Verb + Subject?
    • Example: How are you feeling today?
  • Additional Structures: Questions can be expanded by adding other elements, such as prepositions or adjectives.
    • Example: How long will the meeting last?
  1. Special Features of Usage
  • "What" is open-ended and usually expects a broad answer, while "which" is more specific.
    • Context Example: "What car do you drive?" vs. "Which car is yours — the red or the blue one?"
  • "How" can modify various aspects of a question, such as manner, time, and quantity.
    • Context Examples:
      • How old are you? (age)
      • How far is your house from here? (distance)
      • How well did she perform? (manner)
  1. Examples in Various Situations
  • Daily Conversations:
    • What: What do you do for fun?
    • Which: Which restaurant should we go to tonight?
    • How: How can I help you with this?
  • Professional Settings:
    • What: What are the project goals?
    • Which: Which strategy should we implement?
    • How: How do you propose we proceed?
  • Educational Contexts:
    • What: What subject do you like the most?
    • Which: Which book did you find most helpful?
    • How: How did you prepare for the exam?
  1. Explanation of Usage
  • Why Use These Questions:
    • To gather information effectively and precisely.
    • To clarify specifics in conversation.
  • When to Use:
    • Use "what" when the answer could be anything within a broad category.
    • Use "which" when there is a limited set of options.
    • Use "how" to get information about the way something is done or the state of something.
  • Key Points to Note:
    • Pay attention to the context to choose the right question word.
    • Consider the specificity of your question: use "which" for limited options and "what" for a wider range.
  1. Practice and Tips
  • Practice Questions:
  1. What is your favorite season, and why?
  2. Which of these movies have you seen?
  3. How do you usually spend your weekends?
  • Tips for Mastery:
    • Practice forming questions with these words in different contexts.
    • Focus on the specificity of "which" and the open-ended nature of "what."
    • Use "how" to ask about methods, conditions, and degrees.
