Раздел 1: Грамматика

Подразделы:

  1. Грамматическое правило:
    • Вербалы (Verbals) — это формы глаголов, которые не выступают в роли сказуемого в предложении. К ним относятся инфинитивы, герундии и причастия.
    • Инфинитивы (Infinitives) имеют форму с частицей "to" (например, to run) или без неё (голый инфинитив).
    • Герундий (Gerund) — это форма с окончанием -ing, функционирующая как существительное.
  2. Структура предложений:
    • Инфинитивы: Используются в утвердительных, отрицательных и вопросительных предложениях.
      • Утверждение: I want to learn English.
      • Отрицание: She doesn’t like to drive.
      • Вопрос: Do you need to leave early?
    • Герундий: Также используется во всех типах предложений.
      • Утверждение: I enjoy swimming.
      • Отрицание: He avoids speaking in public.
      • Вопрос: Do you mind helping me?
  1. Особенности использования:
    • Инфинитив используется для выражения цели, намерения или после определённых глаголов.
    • Герундий часто используется после предлогов и определённых глаголов (enjoy, mind, avoid).
  2. Примеры:
    • Инфинитив: I want to improve my skills. / She needs to eat.
    • Герундий: Running is good for health. / They discussed moving abroad.
  3. Когда и почему используется:
    • Инфинитив: Для выражения цели (I came to help), после модальных глаголов и в конструкциях с "let", "make", "hear".
    • Герундий: Для выражения действия как объекта или после предлогов (She is afraid of flying).
  4. Важные моменты:
    • Список глаголов, после которых следует использовать герундий или инфинитив, поможет быстрее освоить тему.
    • Не путайте конструкции: I like to swim (предпочтение) и I like swimming (общее увлечение).
  5. Рекомендации по изучению:
    • Практикуйтесь, составляя предложения с инфинитивом и герундием.
    • Повторяйте примеры и используйте упражнения для лучшего запоминания.

Текст на основе статьи BBC

In August 2014, Sir Cliff Richard's apartment in Berkshire was searched by South Yorkshire Police concerning an alleged historical sexual assault on a boy under 16. The BBC was present during the raid, having been tipped off by the police, and broadcasted the event live, including aerial footage from a helicopter. This unprecedented media coverage led to widespread criticism regarding the collaboration between the police and the media.

Sir Cliff Richard, who was in Portugal at the time, denied the allegations, stating they were "completely false." He expressed distress over the media's presence during the search, highlighting that he had not been informed beforehand. The incident raised questions about the legality and ethics of the police's actions and their relationship with the press.

The BBC received numerous complaints about their coverage, with many accusing them of sensationalism and invasion of privacy. Legal experts and public figures called for an independent inquiry into the police's conduct and their decision to inform the media prior to the search. The Home Affairs Select Committee summoned officials from both the BBC and South Yorkshire Police to address these concerns.

During the committee hearing, the chief constable of South Yorkshire Police accused the BBC of "extortion," a claim that was dismissed by MPs. The committee concluded that the BBC had acted within its rights, but the incident highlighted the need for clearer guidelines on police-media relations. The controversy also sparked a broader debate about the balance between public interest and individual privacy rights.

In June 2016, Sir Cliff Richard was informed that he would not face any charges, leading him to consider legal action against the BBC for their coverage. The BBC apologized for the distress caused but maintained that their reporting was in the public interest. This case underscored the complexities of media responsibility and the potential consequences of premature reporting on sensitive legal matters.

In July 2018, London's High Court ruled in favor of Sir Cliff Richard, awarding him £210,000 in damages for the infringement of his privacy rights by the BBC. The court's decision emphasized the importance of respecting individuals' privacy, even when reporting on matters of public interest. This ruling prompted media organizations to re-evaluate their practices concerning the coverage of ongoing investigations.

The Sir Cliff Richard case serves as a significant example of the challenges faced by media outlets in balancing the public's right to know with the protection of individual privacy. It highlights the necessity for ethical journalism and the careful consideration of the potential impact of media coverage on those involved in legal proceedings.
