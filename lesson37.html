1. Основные правила использования:

    • There is используется с единственными существительными (например, There is a cat in the garden).
    • There are используется с множественными существительными (например, There are three dogs outside).
    • Эти конструкции применяются для выражения существования чего-либо в определённом месте или контексте​

2. Инверсия подлежащего и сказуемого:

    • В конструкции "There is/are" слово there не является подлежащим, а вводит фразу и указывает на существование объекта, который идёт после глагола. Подлежащее идёт после сказуемого, что отличает эту конструкцию от обычного порядка слов​

3. Особенности согласования с рядом существительных:

    • Когда существительные смешанного числа (например, There is a cat and two dogs in the yard), правило меняется. Глагол согласуется с ближайшим существительным (например, There is a cat and two dogs; There are two dogs and a cat)​

Lesson on "There is / There are" (Parts 1, 2, 3)

 

 

Part 1: Basic Usage Rules

    • There is – Use "there is" with singular nouns to indicate the existence of one item or entity:
      • Example: There is a book on the table.
    • There are – Use "there are" with plural nouns for multiple items:
      • Example: There are three books on the table.
    • This construction is often used to describe the presence or location of things or people in a specific place or situation.

 

 

Part 2: Subject-Verb Inversion

    • In sentences with "there is/are", the word there is not the subject; it functions as an introductory element.
    • Subject-verb inversion occurs, meaning the noun that is the real subject appears after the verb be.
      • Example: There is a cat under the table. Here, cat is the actual subject, not there.
    • This inversion can cause confusion but simply remember: the verb (is/are) must agree with the noun that comes after it​

EnglishClub.com​

LearnEnglish - British Council

Part 3: Agreement with Compound Subjects

    • When there is/are is followed by a mixed series (e.g., both singular and plural nouns), the verb usually agrees with the noun that is closest.
      • Example 1: There is a dog and two cats in the yard. (singular is follows closest noun dog)
      • Example 2: There are two cats and a dog in the yard. (plural are follows closest noun cats)
    • This rule, known as proximity agreement, allows flexibility, especially in casual contexts​

Continuing the Lesson on "There is / There are"

 

Part 4: Formal vs. Informal Usage

    • In informal spoken English, it's common to use "there's" even with plural nouns:
      • Example: There's three dogs in the yard.
    • However, in formal writing and speech, it’s recommended to maintain correct subject-verb agreement and use "there are" for plurals:
      • Example: There are three dogs in the yard.
    • Using "there’s" with plurals is widely accepted in conversation but should be avoided in professional or academic settings​

Part 5: Using "There is/are" in Different Tenses

    • There is/are can be used in various tenses to indicate the existence or presence of something in the past, present, or future.
      • Past Tense:
        • Singular: There was a problem with the computer.
        • Plural: There were many people at the event.
      • Future Tense:
        • Singular: There will be a meeting tomorrow.
        • Plural: There will be opportunities for questions at the end.
      • Present Perfect:
        • There has been a mistake.
        • There have been several delays.
    • This flexibility allows speakers to discuss existence and presence across different times​

Part 6: Negative and Interrogative Forms

    • Negative Form:
      • To make a sentence negative, simply add not after "there is/are":
        • Singular: There isn’t a book on the table.
        • Plural: There aren’t any chairs in the room.
    • Interrogative Form:
      • To form a question, switch the order of there and is/are:
        • Singular: Is there a problem?
        • Plural: Are there any questions?
    • This structure helps in both asking questions and giving responses about existence, adding versatility to everyday conversation​

20 Examples of "There is / There are" Across Different Aspects

1. Basic Usage:

    • There is a new exhibit at the museum. (Singular)
    • There are several opportunities for advancement in this field. (Plural)

2. Inverted Subject-Verb Agreement:

    • There is a chance and a responsibility to act now. (Proximity agreement with singular closest noun)
    • There are options and a proposal on the table. (Proximity agreement with plural closest noun)

3. Formal vs. Informal Usage:

    • There are many people interested in the event. (Formal)
    • There’s a lot of cars parked outside. (Informal, spoken English)

4. Different Tenses:

    • Past Tense: 7. There was an issue with the shipment last week. 8. There were numerous complaints after the service update.
    • Future Tense: 9. There will be a meeting next Friday to discuss this. 10. There will be other chances to apply if you’re not ready now.
    • Present Perfect: 11. There has been a mistake in the data calculations. 12. There have been several updates to the guidelines.

5. Negative and Interrogative Forms:

    • Negative: 13. There isn’t any reason to worry about it now. 14. There aren’t enough chairs for everyone in the hall.
    • Interrogative: 15. Is there a possibility we could reschedule? 16. Are there any volunteers for the cleanup crew?

6. Using Mixed Singular and Plural Subjects:

    • There’s a cat and two dogs sleeping on the porch. (Informal, mixed singular and plural)
    • There are three notebooks and a pen on the desk. (Plural-first order)

7. Contractions and Short Forms:

    • There’s only one way to get there on time.
    • There aren’t any easy answers to these questions.
